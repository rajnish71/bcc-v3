Why Boxed — Not Full-Bleed
The full-bleed hero dominated the page. The canonical PageHero allows the photograph to introduce the story without becoming the entire first impression. Whitespace above and beside the frame elevates the image as a framed editorial plate — the same principle that makes photographs feel more significant in a printed magazine.
Canonical Photo Credit Component
The photo credit appears bottom-right within the hero frame, at low opacity on dark overlay. In the body sections, the canonical Photo Credit component appears below each photograph — "Photo by [Name]" with name linking to /photographers/[username]. Consistent across the entire page.

Mission

To build a photography community where every member can learn, grow, and contribute.

Bhopal Camera Club exists for photographers at every stage of the craft — the beginner who just picked up their first camera, and the seasoned practitioner who wants to keep growing. We believe that photography learned in community is photography learned more deeply.

To be the most trusted visual record of Bhopal — a living archive of the city through the eyes of its photographers.

Every photowalk we conduct, every image shared in our gallery, every workshop we run adds another layer to a collective document of Bhopal. Over decades, this archive will be something of genuine historical value to this city.

A City Worth Photographing

Bhopal is one of India's most underrated cities for photography. Its two lakes — Bada Talab and Chota Talab — offer golden-hour reflections that few cities can match. The Upper Lake at dawn, when the light is still low and the water is still, is one of the finest photographic situations in central India.

Van Vihar National Park sits at the edge of the city. Winter brings migratory birds to the Bhoj Wetland. The Old City — dense, alive, historically layered — rewards the street photographer who is willing to walk slowly and look carefully.

Across every season and every light condition, Bhopal offers something exceptional. This is why we have been photographing it for a decade, and why we will still be photographing it a decade from now.

Profile Card — One Component Family
Leadership and Mentors use the same Profile Card. Only the data changes. The component does not know whether it is rendering a coordinator or a mentor — it renders what it receives. This is the definition of a reusable component. The Mentors section below uses identical HTML structure.
Why 6 Coordinators (3+3)
The expanded grid reflects the complete Core Coordinator structure. The V6 02 original showed 3 placeholder cards — Ver2 shows the full 6 as specified in the brief. Row 1: Club Coordinator, Joint Coordinator, Secretary. Row 2: Treasurer, Activity Coordinator, Content Coordinator.
