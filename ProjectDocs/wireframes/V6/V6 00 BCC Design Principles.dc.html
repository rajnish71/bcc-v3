Visual Direction — Refined Editorial Luxury

The V6 direction is confirmed: Refined Editorial Luxury — the hybrid of all three V5 concepts. The visual language name is updated from "Premium Editorial" to "Premium Editorial × Contemporary Luxury", with the resolved design system name "Refined Editorial Luxury" as the canonical identifier.

FROM CONCEPT A — LEICA EDITORIAL
Structural skeleton — hairline rules, asymmetric editorial grid, exhibition-label captions, typographic restraint. Photography presented like prints in a portfolio box.
FROM CONCEPT B — APPLE LUXURY
Material finish — warm ivory satin surfaces, soft layered depth, one metallic-gold CTA per view, gentle light gradients. Interface recedes to let photography lead.
FROM CONCEPT C — PHOTOGRAPHY MAGAZINE
Photography density — full-bleed image moments, justified member gallery grids, category plates. Photography at ≈70% visual share across all pages.
Confirmed V6 Direction

Refined Editorial Luxury

Foundation is typography + hairlines, not effects. Gloss used only as seasoning: one gradient CTA, one satin nav surface, soft shadows ≤ 3 levels. Photography at ≈70% visual share. Natural aspect ratios preserved on all photographs. Will hold up over a 5–10 year horizon.

Photography Philosophy — Natural Aspect Ratio

V6 Photography Rule — Non-Negotiable
DEPRECATED — V5 behaviour
  • ✕ object-fit: cover on all gallery cells
  • ✕ Forced 4:5 aspect ratio on all images
  • ✕ Cropped landscape, portrait, panorama to uniform frame
  • ✕ Fixed-height containers with overflow:hidden
V6 RULE
  • ✓ Every photograph at its original aspect ratio
  • ✓ Never crop photographs. Never use object-fit: cover
  • ✓ Portrait, landscape and panorama supported equally
  • ✓ Justified layouts preferred — same row height, natural widths

Justified layout: Images arranged in rows with a shared target height. Each image's width is proportional to its natural aspect ratio — no cropping, no letterboxing. ImageKit serves the original; the browser computes display width.

Fallback & States — V6

Missing image: --surface-2 plate + monogram logo at 12% opacity + exhibition-label kicker. Maintains layout rhythm without a broken image.
Loading: Satin shimmer on --surface-2 at the natural ratio of the expected image. No spinners.
Hover: Caption bar overlays at bottom of frame — no scale transform. Caption slides up 0.4s cubic.
Mobile (<768px): Justified grid collapses to 2-col then 1-col natural-ratio stack. Hover captions become persistent labels.

Public / Member Separation & Member Hub

MEM-006 P1 — Design Mandate

Identity, Registration, and Membership are independent

Step 1
Registration
Creates identity only. No membership implied. User role. Access to public content.
↓
Step 2
Membership Application
Separate workflow. Form, review, approval. Lifecycle: PENDING → APPROVED → ACTIVE.
↓
Step 3
Membership Active
Full entitlements. Member Hub accessible. ACTIVE state. MEM-007 number assigned.
Design Mandates
  • —Registration creates identity only.
  • —Membership is a separate constitutional process.
  • —Public pages never expose operational member functionality.
  • —Member Hub is accessible only after successful authentication and authorisation.
Design language must reinforce each step as a distinct milestone — never merge or imply automatic progression.
Member Hub — Design Philosophy

Hub as an Independent Application

Definition: The Member Hub is an independent application sharing the same visual language as the public website, while prioritising productivity, workflow efficiency, and member operations over editorial presentation.

Interaction philosophy: The Hub follows a distinct interaction philosophy — task-oriented, sidebar-anchored, and operationally dense — while maintaining complete brand consistency with the public site.

Left-rail navigation: Persistent vertical sidebar anchors the Hub experience. Sections: Overview, My Gallery, Profile, Renewals, Membership Card. Role-elevated sections (Editor, Moderator, Admin) appear below in the sidebar.

Responsive Hub: Below 900px, left rail collapses to a top tab bar. Below 600px, tab bar scrolls horizontally. Hub layout never stacks nav above content on mobile — horizontal scroll tab preferred.

Auth gating: The Member Hub is accessible only after successful authentication and authorisation. Unauthenticated or insufficiently authorised access redirects to the sign-in page, preserving the intended destination.
