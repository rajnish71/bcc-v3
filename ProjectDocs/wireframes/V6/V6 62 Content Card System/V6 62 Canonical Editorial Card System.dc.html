01

Purpose & Scope

The Editorial Card is the atomic unit of content presentation. It is the contract between a single piece of written or documentary content and the user — in every section, for every content type, across the entire platform. All editorial surfaces consume exactly one card component, parameterised by variant and content type.

ED-ARCH-001 — Governing Constraint

One card. Everywhere.

There is no "Activities card", "Projects card", or "News card" at the design level. There is ONE Editorial Card, five variants. Variant selection is driven by layout context and editorial weight — never by page or content category. Any divergence from this architecture must pass design authority review.

Photography-first. Always.

When a cover image is present, it commands the card. It is never cropped, never distorted, never hidden behind text. The image is presented at its natural ratio. Text is a secondary layer — it serves the image, not the reverse. Cards without images use white space as the primary material.

08

Kicker & Content-Type System

The kicker is Zone C — always JetBrains Mono, uppercase, wide-tracked. It signals content type and date. It never carries colour variation between cards in the same grid — uniformity is the rule. The sole exception is the Feature variant, where the kicker inherits --gold-light for legibility on the dark scrim.

Colour Rule — ED-ARCH-003

All kickers on white/ivory surfaces use --gold-600 (#A8843C). There is no colour-coding by content type. The content type signal comes from the kicker text itself, never from colour. Kickers on dark surfaces (Feature scrim) use --gold-light (#C9A961) for legibility only.
